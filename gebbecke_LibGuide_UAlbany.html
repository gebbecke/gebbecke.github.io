    About

    About This Guide

    Need help finding in-depth resources on Impressionism? This guide to the New York Public Library's resources on Impressionism is intended to serve as a starting point for exploratory research focused on the 19th-century art movement. Navigate each tab to find a mix of scholarly and popular works that address various aspects of Impressionism, ranging from comprehensive histories of the movement to cultural analyses of how Impressionist art influenced cultural and aesthetic ideas across the world. 

    Many of the print resources mentioned in this guide can be found in the Library's Art & Architecture Collection, located on the third floor of the Stephen A. Schwarzman Building. See FAQs, Art & Architecture Collection for more information.

    This guide focuses on the Research Catalog and Ex Libris Search catalog. If you are looking for resources that can be checked out at a branch library, please visit the Library's general catalog.

    Research Catalog

    How to Use the Research Catalog

    There are three primary ways to use the Research Catalog:

    (1) Search

    The default Search enables users to search for resources according to six parameters: keywords, title, journal title, author/contributor names, standard numbers, and subject headings. If you are conducting a general search, it's recommended to start here. 

    (2) Subject Heading Explorer/Index

    The Subject Heading Explorer/Index enables users to search for resources that are catalogued under a specific subject heading. Subject headings may be found by either consulting the alphabetic index or searching for them directly. The general subject heading for Impressionism is "Impressionism (Art)."

    (3) Advanced Search

    The Advanced Search enables users to search according to seven parameters: keywords, title, author, subject, language, date range, and format. This option is especially useful for locating resources on Impressionism that were published in a specific language and/or were published during a specific time period.

    Books on Impressionism

    A Note on Call Numbers

    The New York Public Library's research collections use several different call number systems. In general, art-related monographs are classified under JQ, but not all relevant resources may be found under this call number. For this reason, it is recommended to use other search descriptors when looking for new resources. If you know the call number of an existing resource and would like to locate it using the Research Catalog, you can select the "Standard Numbers" field in the search options menu and input the call number to obtain the desired resource.

    Journals & Magazines

    Search Information

    There are two ways to search for journals and magazines:

    (1) Ex Libris Search (For home use) & Ex Libris Search (For on-site use) [Use for digital resources]

    The Ex Libris Search catalog provides direct access to all of the online versions of journals, magazines, and ebooks that are available through the New York Public Library. Some resources will only be available for use by cardholders who visit a branch library in-person, while other resources are also available to cardholders at home. 

    (2) Research Catalog [Use for print resources]

    The Research Catalog contains records for both print and digital versions of library resources. Please note that records are separated by format; i.e., there are at least two separate records for the print version and digital version of each resource. For this reason, we suggest using the Ex Libris Search when searching exclusively for digital resources and using the Research Catalog when searching exclusively for print resources or for both formats at once.

    Journals & Magazines

    Apollo [Print Access | Digital Access]

    Founded in 1925, Apollo Magazine publishes essays, book reviews, and exhibition reviews relating to a wide variety of art history topics, including Impressionism. Apollo is a great resource for keeping up-to-date on new exhibitions and art-related events across the world. Issues are published monthly. 

    The Art Bulletin [Print Access | Digital Access]

    Founded in 1913, The Art Bulletin is a long-established journal for scholarly work on art history in general. Issues are published quarterly.

    Art History [Print Access | Digital Access]

    Founded in 1978, Art History is a journal focused on new and innovative approaches to art history scholarship. Issues are published five times a year.

    The Burlington Magazine [Print Access | Digital Access]

    Founded in 1903, The Burlington Magazine is one of the world's oldest periodicals focused on art and art history. Issues are published monthly.

    Oxford Art Journal [Print Access | Digital Access]

    Founded in 1978, the Oxford Art Journal publishes scholarly work on art and art history, with an emphasis on research that aims to reexamine current disciplinary standards and methodologies. Issues are published three times a year. 

    General Reference Resources

    Search Information

    One quick way to locate general reference works using the Research Catalog is to navigate to the Advanced Search function and specify the kind of reference work you are looking for as one of the search keywords. For example, to search for dictionaries focused on art history terminology, you can specify "dictionary" and "art history" as keywords when conducting a search.

    Another strategy for locating general reference works is to use the Subject Heading Explorer/Index. For example, to search for encyclopedias about Impressionism, you can use the subject heading "Impressionism (Art) -- Encyclopedias." Please note that the Subject Heading Explorer is sensitive to term order when locating valid subject headings; e.g., searching for "Encyclopedias" and "Impressionism" (in that order) will not locate any subject headings, while a search for "Impressionism" and "Encyclopedias" (in that order) will locate the subject heading "Impressionism (Art) -- Encyclopedias."

    Digital Resources

    The Grove Encyclopedia of American Art

    The Grove Encyclopedia of American Art is a useful resource for conducting exploratory research on Impressionism in the context of American art. 

    The Oxford Dictionary of Art and Artists (5th edition)

    The Oxford Dictionary of Art and Artists (5th edition) is the most recent edition of Oxford's general-purpose art dictionary. [Note: The Oxford Dictionary of Art was renamed The Oxford Dictionary of Art and Artists during the publication of the fourth edition. NYPL does not currently own this edition in print.]

    The Oxford Companion to Western Art

    The Oxford Companion to Western Art is a substantial reference work that encompasses over 1700 artists associated with art movements throughout history. Its extensive scope makes it a great resource for finding information on artists who may not be included in other reference sources. 

    Databases

    Search Information

    General Database Search

    To obtain a list of all of the NYPL's databases relating to art and art history, navigate to the filter option labeled "Subjects" and select either "Art," "Art History," or both, then click "Apply Filters."

    Databases

    Allgemeines Künstlerlexikon (AKL)

    Allgemeines Künstlerlexikon (AKL), or Artists of the World Online, provides comprehensive biographical information on artists from across the world and from different time periods throughout history. 

    Art & Architecture Source

    Art & Architecture Source facilitates access to international journals focused on art, art history, and architecture. Art & Architecture Source also provides access to thousands of images and art reproductions. 

    ARTBibliographies Modern

    ARTBibliographies Modern indexes journal articles, books, and other resources focused on art and art history from the late 19th-century onward. The broad scope of this database makes it a great starting point for research on Impressionism. If you encounter difficulty finding relevant material on your topic of interest, try using search operators such as "AND" and "OR" to experiment with different combinations of terms (e.g., try this search query to find information about Impressionism in a French or American context: Impressionism AND (French OR American)).

    NYARC Discovery

    NYARC Discovery enables users to search a large subset of the collective library collections of the Brooklyn Museum, The Frick Collection, and The Museum of Modern Art, which together form the New York Art Resources Consortium. This database supports searching by title, author/artist, subject, and WorldCat number.

    Oxford Art Online

    Oxford Art Online is an all-in-one database that provides access to Grove Art Online, the Benezit Dictionary of Artists, the Encyclopedia of Aesthetics, The Oxford Companion to Western Art, and The Concise Oxford Dictionary of Art Terms. Please note that the search function for this database will primarily show results for Grove Art Online and the Benezit Dictionary of Artists only; however, results from the latter three reference sources can be found by conducting a preliminary search and clicking on the appropriate link at the top of the search results (i.e., "Search for 'Search Phrase' in The Concise Oxford Dictionary of Art Terms, Encyclopedia of Aesthetics, and Oxford Companion to Western Art"). 

    Useful Websites

    Art History Websites, Museums, and More

     Smarthistory

    Smarthistory is an educational nonprofit organization that provides an assortment of essays and videos created by art history experts.

     National Gallery of Art

    The National Gallery of Art is an important repository of Impressionist artworks. One of the strengths of the NGA's collection search functionality is that the results can be sorted according to artistic style. Try using the "Styles" filter and selecting "Impressionist" in order to view all of the Impressionist artworks held by the museum.

     The Metropolitan Museum of Art

    The Met Museum has a large collection of Impressionist artworks and offers a variety of educational resources relating to art history, such as the Heilbrun Timeline of Art History. 

     

      Getty Art and Architecture Thesaurus

    The Getty Art & Architecture Thesaurus is a beneficial resource for anyone seeking authoritative definitions for concepts relating to art and Impressionism.

    Google Arts and Culture

    Google Arts and Culture is an excellent tool for exploring artworks and taking virtual tours of museums around the world. Visit this page for an overview of Impressionism and access to collections of artworks created by artists connected to the Impressionist movement.

     Perspective Arts (YouTube Channel)

    The Perspective Arts YouTube Channel includes several detailed documentaries about art history and Impressionism produced by art critic Waldemar Januszczak.
